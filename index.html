PROTOCOL COMPLIANCE BREAKDOWN

Observed Strengths

  • Rapid initial recognition of cardiac arrest and pulse check performed within 10s.
  • Exceptional chest compression rate, averaging 112 compressions per minute.
  • Effective team leadership and clear closed-loop communication commands.

Protocol Deviations / Area for Growth

  • Delay in CPR restart: Pause in chest compressions during defibrillator charging cycle was 14s (Guideline threshold: < 10s).
  • Ventilation over-volume: 2 bag-valve ventilations exceeded the target tidal volume (visible hyperinflation).

Actionable Coaching Recommendations

  1. Practice defibrillator pad pre-charging while chest compressions are actively occurring. Do not stop compressions while the unit charges; compressions should only halt for rhythm check and shock delivery.
  2. Conduct BVM ventilation practice on a volume-feedback trainer to gain muscle memory for appropriate chest rise without hyperventilating the patient.

Assessor Final Comments

Overall solid team leadership and command of the room. Compressions were at the correct rate. However, the interruption in chest compressions during defibrillator preparation exceeded the 10-second limit. We must coordinate pads prep and charging while compressions are running.
